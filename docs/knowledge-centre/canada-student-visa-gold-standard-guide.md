## 1 · Overview  🟦

**Purpose** — Orient counsellor and client on the service and the journey.

**Counsellor guidance**
- Service = guiding a client to a **Canadian study permit from outside Canada** before travel.
- Future Link's role: **advisory, preparation, readiness** — never the government decision.
- Who it's for: students with (or seeking) a DLI offer who plan to study in Canada.

**Future Link recommendations**
- Lead the first session with **fit and goals**, not paperwork.
- State plainly: we raise *quality and readiness*; **IRCC decides**.

**Expected outcome** — A well-prepared, honest application + a client ready to settle.

**Counselling objective** → *The client understands what this service is, what Future Link does, and that approval rests with IRCC.*

**Content classification:** Future Link Content 🟦 · Official Reference (overview link) 🔗

## 9 · Common Mistakes  🟦

**Purpose** — Prevent the errors that cause refusals and stress.

**Counsellor guidance** — Coach against these proactively; review at journey stages 5, 7, 11.

1. Treating Future Link as a **guarantee** — reset expectations.
2. Funds not **liquid** or arranged late.
3. Ignoring **PAL/TAL** need for the category.
4. **Not disclosing** a prior refusal.
5. Weak, generic **SOP / study plan**.
6. Unexplained **program/level switches**.
7. Quebec client **missing CAQ** awareness.
8. Booking **travel before the decision**.
9. Leaving **family planning** too late.
10. Ignoring **PGWP implications** at program selection.

**How counsellors prevent them** — Keep this list in every file; address each at the relevant stage.

**Counselling objective** → *The client avoids the predictable, high-cost mistakes before they happen.*

**Content classification:** Future Link Content 🟦
