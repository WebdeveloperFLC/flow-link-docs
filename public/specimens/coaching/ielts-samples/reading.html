Passage excerpt — Urban green spaces

FLC training specimen. True / False / Not Given format.

Passage

Cities worldwide are investing in parks and tree-lined streets to improve air quality and public health. Research from several European cities suggests that residents living within 300 metres of a green space report lower stress levels than those farther away. However, not all neighbourhoods benefit equally: older districts with limited land often have fewer parks, and maintenance budgets vary by municipality.

Some planners argue that rooftop gardens and vertical greenery can compensate where ground space is scarce. Critics note that such projects require ongoing irrigation and structural support, which may not be sustainable in every climate. Despite these debates, most urban policy documents now list green infrastructure as a long-term goal rather than a optional amenity.

Questions 1–5

Do the following statements agree with the information in the passage?

Write TRUE if the statement agrees · FALSE if it contradicts · NOT GIVEN if there is no information

1. People near green spaces may experience less stress.  
2. Every city district has an equal number of parks.  
3. Rooftop gardens always cost less than ground parks.  
4. Maintenance funding differs between municipalities.  
5. All urban plans now treat green infrastructure as essential long-term.  

Answer key

1 TRUE · 2 FALSE · 3 NOT GIVEN · 4 TRUE · 5 TRUE
