Description

A Canada study permit authorizes international students to study full-time at a Designated Learning Institution (DLI). This service covers first-time applicants applying from outside Canada before travel—typically online through an IRCC secure account—with biometrics, medical, PAL/TAL, or Quebec CAQ where applicable.

Who should use this pathway

Students with a valid LOA from a Canadian DLI who will apply from their country of residence (not already in Canada as a visitor seeking status change). Counselors assess SDS vs Non-SDS, funds, academics, and genuine student intent before submission.

Application streams

Student Direct Stream (SDS): for residents of eligible countries who meet upfront language, GIC, tuition payment, and medical requirements—generally faster processing. Non-SDS: standard study permit for all other eligible applicants; requires strong funds history and complete documentation.

Key authority

Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC). Applications are assessed by the visa office responsible for the applicant's country of residence. CBSA issues the physical study permit at the port of entry.

After approval

Client receives a port of entry (POE) letter of introduction and, if required, a temporary resident visa (TRV) vignette. The study permit is printed at entry. Counselor must brief the client on enrollment deadlines, permit conditions, work limits, address updates, and PGWP planning.

Pre-qualify the client; official rules are linked in Sources.

  • Letter of acceptance (LOA) from a DLI core/met
    Purpose: proves admission to a real program. Importance: core eligibility—officer will not approve without it. Common mistakes: expired LOA, wrong DLI number, start date already passed. Best practice: verify DLI on canada.ca; match LOA program, dates, and tuition to the application and SOP.
  • Valid passport (6+ months beyond intended stay recommended) core/met
    Purpose: identity and travel document for permit length. Importance: permit cannot exceed passport validity. Common mistakes: soon-to-expire passport, name mismatch with LOA. Best practice: renew passport before applying if validity is short.
  • Proof of financial support (tuition + living costs) core/met
    Purpose: shows the student can pay for study and living without unauthorized work. Importance: top refusal factor after genuineness. Common mistakes: recent unexplained deposits, only savings with no income trail. Best practice: 4–6 months statements, tuition receipts, GIC for SDS; document source of every large credit.
  • Parent or sponsor financial documents (if sponsored) conditional
    Purpose: links sponsor capacity to student funds. Importance: officers match deposits to sponsor income. Common mistakes: sponsor ITR missing, no relationship proof, funds in sponsor account with no affidavit. Best practice: ITR 2–3 years, employment letter, bank statements, affidavit, and clear explanation of why sponsor pays.
  • Language proficiency evidence (SDS or DLI requirement) conditional
    Purpose: shows ability to succeed in program and meets SDS stream rules. Importance: mandatory for SDS; many DLIs require IELTS/TOEFL/PTE even on Non-SDS. Common mistakes: expired test, wrong module (GT vs Academic), score below SDS threshold. Best practice: confirm DLI minimum and SDS country list before booking test.
  • Study plan / statement of purpose (SOP) conditional
    Purpose: explains why Canada, why this course, why now, and career plan. Importance: central to genuine student assessment. Common mistakes: generic template, course unrelated to past study/work, no gap explanation. Best practice: customized SOP linking academics, work history, and post-study intent; address any refusals or gaps directly.
  • Academic transcripts, degrees, and resume core/met
    Purpose: establishes education background and progression. Importance: supports course fit and credibility. Common mistakes: missing backlogs explanation, inconsistent dates vs forms. Best practice: certified copies, backlog/credential explanation letter where needed.
  • Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL/TAL) if required conditional
    Purpose: provincial allocation for many post-secondary study permits. Importance: application may be incomplete without it unless exempt. Common mistakes: applying before DLI issues PAL, wrong province. Best practice: confirm with DLI whether PAL/TAL is issued and upload before submission.
  • Quebec Acceptance Certificate (CAQ) for Quebec studies conditional
    Purpose: Quebec approval before IRCC can finalize permit. Importance: mandatory for most Quebec programs. Common mistakes: submitting to IRCC before CAQ approval. Best practice: obtain CAQ first, then complete IRCC study permit application.
  • Biometrics completed after BIL conditional
    Purpose: identity verification. Importance: file may stall if BIL expires. Common mistakes: missing 30-day window, no VAC appointment proof. Best practice: book VAC immediately after BIL; keep receipt and upload confirmation.
  • Immigration medical exam (if required) conditional
    Purpose: admissibility screening. Importance: required for SDS upfront medical and for many residence histories. Common mistakes: non-panel physician, expired eMedical. Best practice: use IRCC panel physician; upload IMM 1017B/upfront sheet as instructed.
  • Genuine student intent and home-country ties core/met
    Purpose: officer must believe applicant will leave Canada when authorized stay ends (dual intent allowed but plan must be credible). Importance: refusal if study appears pretext for migration. Common mistakes: weak ties, employment gap unexplained. Best practice: document family, property, job offer potential, or career return plan in India/home country.

Applicant (student)

⏱ Up to 24 hrs/week off-campus in term; full-time in scheduled breaks; on-campus anytime

Study permit holders may work on-campus anytime. Off-campus work is allowed up to 24 hours per week during regular academic sessions and full-time during scheduled breaks, subject to permit conditions.

Details
  • Co-op work requires a co-op work permit in addition to study permit.
  • Work must not start before permit conditions allow — verify printed remarks.
  • PGWP is a separate application after eligible program completion.
Restrictions
  • Cannot work for ineligible employers if restricted on permit
  • Must remain enrolled and making progress at DLI

Spouse (SOWP)

⏱ Full-time — no fixed weekly cap (open permit)

A spouse/common-law partner may qualify for a Spouse Open Work Permit (SOWP). It is an OPEN permit — they can work full-time for almost any employer, with NO fixed weekly hour limit (unlike the student's 24 hrs/week). Eligibility is narrowed: only spouses of students in a master's (16+ months), doctoral, or specified professional program qualify.

Details
  • Open permit: full-time, almost any employer, no LMIA, no job offer needed.
  • No weekly hour cap on the spouse (the 24 hrs/week limit applies to the student, not the SOWP holder).
  • Eligibility narrowed (since 21 Jan 2025): undergraduate/college/diploma study no longer qualifies the spouse.
  • As of 4 Mar 2026, applications are refused if the student is in their final academic term.
  • Validity is usually tied to the student's study permit; partner applies separately.
Restrictions
  • Not automatic — confirm the student's program is on the eligible list before promising spouse work rights.
  • Final-term students: SOWP will be refused (new applications and renewals).

  • Match course choice to prior education or credible career progression
  • Explain study gaps in SOP with supporting documents
  • Use seasoned funds with ITR-linked sponsor evidence
  • Submit tuition receipts and organized bank statements
  • Disclose and address all prior refusals in writing

  • Do not lodge SDS without every SDS requirement met

  • Do not use expired LOA or wrong tuition on forms
  • Do not hide refusals or misrepresent employment history
  • Do not quote IRCC fees without checking the current fee page

  • Copy-paste SOP with wrong college or program name

Standard answers. Confirm figures on canada.ca.

Who can apply for a Canada Study Permit from outside Canada?

A foreign national with a valid LOA from a DLI who meets financial, admissibility, and genuine-student requirements. They apply from their country of residence (or as IRCC instructs) before travel. They must be able to study full-time, pay tuition and living costs, and comply with temporary resident rules.

What financial documents should a first-time applicant show?

Typically: 4–6 months bank statements, tuition payment receipts or ability to pay first year, proof of living funds per IRCC guidelines, sponsor ITR and employment letters if parents pay, GIC certificate for SDS, and affidavits explaining large deposits. Every credit should be traceable to lawful income or assets.

Can parents sponsor a student's study permit application?

Yes—most Indian students are sponsored by parents or close family. IRCC expects relationship proof, sponsor income evidence (ITR, salary slips), bank statements, and a clear statement of why the sponsor is paying. Funds should align with sponsor capacity, not just account balance.

How much money must a student demonstrate?

At minimum: first-year tuition (per LOA) plus living expenses (IRCC publishes annual amounts—e.g. CAD 20,635+ for one person outside Quebec, updated periodically). SDS also requires GIC and upfront tuition payment per stream rules. Always verify current amounts on canada.ca before quoting.

Are study gaps acceptable?

Yes, if explained credibly. Gaps should be covered in the SOP and supporting documents (employment letters, business records, caregiving, health, or upskilling). Unexplained multi-year gaps without activity are a common refusal reason.

What are the most common refusal reasons for study permits?

Insufficient or unseasoned funds, weak genuine-student/SOP, course mismatch, undisclosed prior refusals, missing PAL/CAQ, and poor document consistency. Counselors should pre-screen these before submission.

Can a spouse accompany the student to Canada?

Often yes, but the spouse needs their own application (visitor record or open work permit if eligible under current SOWP rules). Extra funds and relationship evidence are required. Assess SOWP eligibility by program level and duration before promising work rights.

Can children accompany the student?

Yes—minor children may need study permits or visitor records depending on age and schooling plans. Each dependant needs funds, medical, and biometrics as applicable. Family applications must be consistent on forms and finances.

Is IELTS mandatory for a Canada study permit?

Not for every Non-SDS file, but mandatory for SDS (minimum scores per IRCC). Most DLIs require English/French proof for admission. Counselors should confirm both DLI requirements and SDS thresholds before advising 'no IELTS.'

Can a student work while studying in Canada?

Eligible full-time students at participating DLIs may work on-campus anytime and off-campus up to 24 hours per week during regular academic sessions (full-time in scheduled breaks), if permit conditions allow. Co-op requires a separate co-op work permit. Verify current IRCC work rules at counseling.

What happens after graduation?

After completing an eligible program at a DLI, the graduate may apply for a Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP), then potentially permanent residence through Express Entry or PNP. PGWP length depends on program duration. Plan this pathway in counseling but do not guarantee PR.

Can a student apply after a previous refusal?

Yes, with a stronger file. Disclose all prior refusals on IMM forms, attach a cover letter addressing each refusal reason, and show material change (new funds, course, improved SOP, or corrected documents). Re-submitting unchanged documents usually fails again.

How does IRCC evaluate funds and source of money?

Officers look at sufficiency (tuition + living), accessibility, history (seasoning), and whether deposits match sponsor income or applicant earnings. Large recent transfers without documentation are heavily scrutinized.

What makes a strong study plan or SOP?

Clear link between past education/work and chosen program; specific reasons for Canada and that DLI; realistic career outcome; explanation of gaps and refusals; temporary intent with credible home ties. It must read personal—not copied from the internet.

Which documents matter most for approval?

LOA from DLI, financial package (statements + sponsor ITR), SOP, academics, PAL/CAQ if required, language scores for SDS/DLI, and consistent IMM forms. Weak finances or generic SOP can undermine an otherwise complete file.

SDS vs Non-SDS—which pathway should we use?

Use SDS only if the applicant resides in an SDS-eligible country and meets language, GIC, tuition payment, and medical requirements. Otherwise use Non-SDS with strong seasoned funds and full documentation. Never label a file SDS without meeting every requirement.

When must biometrics be completed?

After receiving the Biometric Instruction Letter (BIL), usually within 30 days. Book the nearest VAC immediately. Missing the window can delay or jeopardize processing.

Is a medical exam required for every applicant?

No. Required for SDS upfront medical, certain countries of residence, or long stays. Follow IRCC medical instructions in the applicant's document checklist—only panel physicians count.

What if the LOA start date is approaching or has passed?

Apply urgently with explanation. If the intake date has passed, obtain a deferral or new LOA from the DLI before submission. Stale LOAs raise credibility concerns.

What is a Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL) and when is it needed?

A provincial document for many post-secondary study permit applicants showing allocation under intake caps. Exemptions exist (e.g. certain levels, family already in Canada). Confirm with DLI whether PAL/TAL is issued before lodging.

Does Quebec require a CAQ before IRCC can approve?

Yes for most studies in Quebec. The student obtains CAQ from Quebec immigration first, then applies to IRCC for the study permit. Do not submit incomplete Quebec files.

What government fees apply for a study permit from India?

Study permit application fee CAD $150 plus biometrics CAD $85 for most applicants (verify on IRCC fee page). Medical, VAC premium services, and courier are additional. Quote separately from consultancy fees.

How long does IRCC processing usually take?

Varies by country and stream—SDS is often faster where eligible; Non-SDS commonly several weeks to a few months. Check IRCC processing times tool for the visa office handling India/residence country. Published times are estimates, not guarantees before intake.

What happens at the port of entry after approval?

Client carries POE letter, LOA, funds proof, and approval documents. CBSA officer interviews and, if satisfied, prints the study permit. Client must check permit conditions (school, work, expiry) before leaving the counter.

Can dependants apply together with the principal student?

Yes—family can be linked in planning, but each person needs correct forms, fees, biometrics, and financial support showing ability to support the whole family in Canada.

What if funds were recently deposited into the bank account?

Explain every deposit with source documents (property sale, FD break, loan, gift deed). Without proof, officers may refuse for unseasoned funds. Advise clients to plan finances months before applying.

How should we address a prior Canada or other-country refusal?

Full disclosure on forms, copy of refusal letter, and a written explanation of what changed (new course, stronger funds, corrected documents). Quality team should review all refusal cases before re-lodgement.

Is a co-op or internship program allowed on a standard study permit?

Academic programs with mandatory co-op need a co-op work permit in addition to the study permit. Confirm with DLI whether co-op is integral and apply for the correct permit.

What conditions must the student maintain after arrival?

Enroll at DLI, make academic progress, respect work limits on permit, notify IRCC of address changes, and not overstay. Dropping to part-time or changing schools without following IRCC rules can affect future PGWP and status.

When should consultancy fees be collected versus lodging the application?

Follow office policy: typically agreement and initial fee at engagement, document milestone payments during file build, and balance before or at IRCC submission. Never promise approval in fee discussions.

  • Paid representation in Canada may require RCIC/L lawyer—confirm who signs client agreement
  • Client service agreement and consent must be on file before submission
  • Never guarantee visa approval; use approved-language only in marketing
  • Fee quotes must separate consultancy, government, and third-party (GIC, medical, biometrics)
